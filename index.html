The Practice

Data, technology, and business strategy.

Senior-level expertise across all three focus areas, delivered by a single principal with two decades of technology leadership, data platform, and business strategy experience.

Dorian Insights is a technology consultancy at the intersection of data and analytics, business strategy, and technology architecture. Josh Andrews is a principal with two decades of experience gained at Tier 1 consulting firms and inside multiple Fortune 500 clients.

The practice is based on several foundational ideas.

  • Analytics and the decision making power it provides is not solely nor even primarily a technical problem. It is an organization-wide issue relating to all aspects of strategic decision-making, structure, authority, responsibility, and communication. All of these must be taken into account for a successful project.
  • One or a few highly skilled and motivated individuals can produce better results, faster, than larger, less skilled teams. This is particularly true with LLMs able to do significant lower-level work.
  • Both a big-picture viewpoint and detailed, ground-up hands-on technical understanding of execution are important and necessary for success.

    • Throughout, the emphasis is on enabling your existing team — building durable, in-house capability that outlasts the engagement.

    Engagement

    Three ways to work together.

    Pick the shape that matches what you need now. Engagements can — and often do — change form as the work evolves.

    Fractional retainer

    Embedded senior presence

    Ongoing committed hours — consistent leadership without a full-time hire.

    • Dedicated weekly hours reserved for the engagement
    • Predictable monthly investment, billed against committed hours
    • Highest priority access and continuity
    • Minimum 3-month term; preferred rates at longer terms
    Best for ongoing leadership needs — data team leadership, platform stewardship, embedded principal architect.
    Project-based

    Defined scope, defined outcome

    Time and materials against clearly defined deliverables and milestones.

    • Scope, milestones, acceptance criteria agreed upfront
    • Proposals include timeline and investment range
    • Scope adapts as findings surface — data work always surprises
    • Honest re-baselining when the work calls for it
    Best for specific initiatives — platform builds, dbt overhauls, cost optimization, modernization roadmaps.
    Advisory

    Targeted, burst availability

    Block of hours for architecture reviews, ad hoc analysis, or advisory calls.

    • Hourly or block-of-hours, purchased in advance or monthly
    • Typical block: 10–20 hours used over 4–8 weeks
    • No long-term commitment required
    • Scheduling subject to current availability
    Best for targeted, flexible engagements — second opinions, decision support, short-form analysis.
